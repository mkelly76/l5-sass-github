Our Day at Joshua Tree National Park

We were able to find one #geocache before losing cell service in the park!

We happened to go on my nephew Joshua's birthday, so I had to send him a Birthday Snap!

Hidden Valley

Our first hike of the day, and one of the most popular. It is a 1.6 km trail that winds among massive boulders through what was believed to be a legendary cattle rustlers' hideout.

Barker Dam

This 1.8 km trail will bring you to Barker Dam, also known as the Big Horn Dam. Barker Dam is a water storage reservoir, constructed by early cattlemen in 1900.

Keys View

This popular destination, perched on the crest of the Little San Bernardino Mountains, provides panoramic views of the Coachella Valley, San Andreas Fault, and on a really clear day you might be able to see Signal Mountain in Mexico.

Hall of Horrors

This 1 km trail takes you by a boulder pile containing slot canyons.

Skull Rock

Skull Rock is located right on the main road that runs through the park, and it certainly looks like an eerie skull with two sunken eye sockets.

Arch Rock

This 2.25 km hike has no shade, and should be started before 10am; not saved for the last hike of the day like we did! LOL Seeing the Arch Rock was so worth it, and we even trekked on further to see the next rock, which was what I was most looking forward to all day...

Heart Rock

This naturally formed granite boulder is located 0.5 km off a spur trail from the Arch Rock Trail. This was our longest and hottest hike of the day, and I am so glad I pushed through! ❤

Cholla Gardens

We did not do the hike at this stop, in fact Darryl didn't even get out of the car. I'm surprised I was able to get out long enough to take this picture of the cacti before realizing just how many bees were swarming around. I couldn't get back into the car fast enough!
